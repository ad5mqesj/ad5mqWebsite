AD5MQ H.F. Transceiver #1

After moving (in 2008), my first serious project has been a full featured, all band HF Transceiver.  I began with a fairly conventional design of the analog circuitry: a SA612 (replaced the NE602) first mixer, MC1350 IF amp, and an SA612 BFO mixer.  I Initially designed a 2N5109 RF amplifier.  This classic design has excellent IP-3 and good gain, but in this case the power consumption and noise figure caused me to replace it with a more modern OP-AMP based design.  I used a Linear Technology video amplifier instead with excellent results.  The IF is 45 MHz- dictated by the availability of nice monolithic crystal filters from ECS (via Digikey).  The fixed BFO is a classic low noise Colpitts design with a varacter that allows for fine adjustment.  The VFO is an Analog Devices 9852 DDS.  the whole thing is controlled by a fairly high end PIC microcontroller, which allowed me to write the software in C, and still have a full suite of features including a keyer.  A late addition was a switched capacitor filter in the audio path - this has its clock supplied by one of the PIC's PWM outputs so its corner frequency can be varied in software.  The current version uses 800 Hz for CW operation and 2.85KHz for SSB.  The RF power amplifier is a salvaged unit I got from eBay - it is capable of 100+W on all bands.  The transceiver is not restricted in software to transmit only in the ham bands so some care is required when operating it.  I had my first on air contact with AA8R in Michigan on November 30 using PSK-31 on 20m at 10 Watts, got a 599 signal report. I monitored the transmitted audio locally with my K2 with no antenna connected just from leakage - it sounded fine.  I am still working on the software, in particular it needs better filtering of the inputs from the main tuning shaft encoder - its quite jumpy.  It has a round off error when computing the tuning word for the DDS which results in a frequency error of up to 725 Hz in some places, I'm sure I can think of other things to improve as well.  Here are some pictures.

Here are the detailed schematic of the main board as a pdf analog.pdf  and a zip Analog_radio.zip of the source code for the processor.  Since all the routines are in C they are pretty readable and might be useful for someone else.  If you look closely at the analog section you'll see that its a direct ripoff of the design in the recent QST by KD1JV.  Since he did such a nice job I reused it rather than roll yet another variant of the same basic circuit.  Though, there are many changes to his basic design such as the use of an Analog Devices video switch to steer the oscillators, the rf amplifiers, (pre and post) and some others.
